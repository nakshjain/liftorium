# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo gym-goers who lift weights and track their own progress. They use Liftorium phone-in-hand between sets — sweaty, distracted, one-handed, standing in a rack. The job: log sets fast, see what they did last time, and get out of the way so they can train.

## Product Purpose

A workout logging tool that makes set entry faster than pen-and-paper. Tracks live sessions with rest timers, references previous performance, and organizes training into weekly plans. Success looks like: the user opens the app, starts logging, and never feels friction between intention and action.

## Brand Personality

Motivating, bold, energetic. Feels like a training partner who's all business once the bar is loaded — pushes you forward without being loud or needy. Direct language, confident UI, zero fluff.

## Anti-references

- Bloated fitness apps (MyFitnessPal-style overload: ads, nutrition tabs, social feeds, upsells, endless nav depth)
- Gamified apps (badge-heavy, streak-obsessed, childish reward loops like Duolingo-for-fitness)

## Design Principles

1. **Fastest path to the bar** — Every screen optimizes for the fewest taps to complete the primary action. If a workflow adds a step, it must earn that step.
2. **Show, don't decorate** — Information density over visual filler. Data the lifter needs (last session's weight, set count, rest time) is always visible, never hidden behind a tap.
3. **Gym-proof** — Designed for sweaty hands, bright overhead lights, and divided attention. Large targets, high contrast, forgiving touch areas.
4. **Momentum, not friction** — The UI should feel like it's pulling you forward through a session, not waiting for input. Active states, timers, and progressive feedback keep energy up.
5. **Earned simplicity** — Fewer features done exceptionally well. Every element on screen justifies its presence or gets cut.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum across all surfaces
- Extra-large touch targets (minimum 48px, prefer 56px+ for primary actions) — gym gloves, sweaty fingers, one-handed use
- High contrast mode support for bright gym lighting conditions
- Reduced motion alternative for all animations
- No color-only state indicators
